#📊 Cross‑Country Economic Insights (2015–2022)
Exploring the Relationship Between Education, Employment, and Economic Outcomes Across OECD Nations
📌 Overview
This Power BI project analyzes how education attainment, employment distribution, and GDP per capita interact across five OECD countries between 2015 and 2022.
The goal is to understand how higher education levels and skilled employment contribute to stronger economic performance.

The report is structured into four interactive pages:

Summary

Education Attainment – Global Trends

Employment Status

Economic Outcomes (GDP)

📂 Datasets Used
All datasets were sourced from the OECD Data Explorer:

Adults’ Educational Attainment Distribution  
(By age group and gender)

Employment by Activities and Status (ALFS)  
(Employment type, gender, and sector distribution)

NAAG Chapter 2: Income  
(GDP per capita and income indicators)

🧠 Key Insights
🎓 Education
Bachelor’s degree attainment increased significantly across countries, with the United States leading in 2022.

Bachelor’s attainment grew by 124% globally between 2015–2022.

Gender distribution remained balanced, with females slightly higher in tertiary education.

👥 Employment
The United States recorded the highest total employment across the period.

Tertiary sector employment dominated in all countries, reflecting modern service‑driven economies.

Germany showed the highest share of employees vs. self‑employed.

💰 Economic Outcomes
The United Kingdom recorded the highest GDP per capita in the selected period.

Countries with higher tertiary education levels tended to show stronger GDP performance.

GDP growth patterns varied significantly during 2020 due to global disruptions.
